Overview

Purpose

It's a common misconception that because someone seems to have perfect vision they don't need eye exams or glasses, but there are several conditions that can affect learning and reading and are largely undetectable except through a cover test and near-point checking during an eye exam, and would be easily treated with reading glasses or eye exercises. This website will help raise awareness on these concerns, as well as teach people about common injuries that I see happening more often to kids than adults due to the nature of the injury -- most frequently hyphemas from nerf guns.

Audience

The intended audience would be primarily parents, especially those who have kids in grade school or in college, but teachers and educators as well would benefit from the knowledge. It may also interest individuals who are hoping to gain a greater understanding of the eye and the lesser known anomalies of vision in relation to learning and development.

Home page

Most people won’t argue when you say that any parent or teacher’s primary concern is the health and well-being of a child, and that eyes are essential for development. However, most people also don’t think about how connected both of those things are! At EyeLearn we hope to raise awareness for the things that people oftentimes don’t know, and wouldn’t know unless you’ve either attended Medical School or Optometry School, that can hurt kids either physically or academically.

Vision and the Classroom

80% of what we learn comes from vision, and recently studies have shown that 60% of "problem-learners" have an undiagnosed eye disorder. How did that happen? Because school screenings only focus on distance vision. Nearsightedness, meaning things at a distance appear blurred but clearer closer-up, poses the biggest risk to learning, there are still many vision disorders that happen at near-vision whic can still greatly impede learning, and tend to be much harder to detect. This is why taking your child for an eye exam before they start school is so important -- just because they can see clearly at a distance doesn't mean they don't have a problem up close.
(Exophoria/Esophoria: Muscle imbalance, fixed with either prism reading glasses or eye exercises. Intermittent, not visible without cover test)
(Astigmatism: A type of refractive error; discuss corneal shape and how astigmatism refracts onto retina. Only fixable through glasses.)

Kids Eye Safety


It seems like kids are always finding ways to hurt themselves. Many of these ways are minor, like soap or dirt in the eye, but others can be serious and even permanently damage their vision. There are even some injuries that are suprisingly common but virtually unheard of by anhone other than those who work with eyes. Knowing about these various injuries helps not only to prevent them but also gives you the knowledge you need in order to treat them if they do happen.
(Hyphemas: Solely caused by trauma. Immediate medical attention. Symptoms include photophobia, terrible pain, possible nausea. Blood may be visible at bottom of anterior chamber)
(Corneal Abrasions: "Rug burn" of the cornea, mostly heals itself with time but can be incredibly painful. Patch and sleep if pain is light (CL patch for older kids), otherwise medical attention. Can lead to eye infections.
(Black Eyes: Cold compresses. Medical attention is highly recommended due to possible consequences, including retinal detachment and orbital fractures. Symptoms to be aware of: black spots, floaters, flashing lights.)
(Foreign Bodies: Wash with saline solution, NOT TAP WATER. If "loose", meaning on the white part of the eye and not embedded, can be removed with damp q-tip. Otherwise get medical attention.)
